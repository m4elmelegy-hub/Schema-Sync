2) SALES ACCOUNTING
===================

When a product is sold:

A) Revenue side:

* Use sale price in sales invoice
* Customer/Safe is debited
* Sales Revenue is credited

B) Cost side:

* Inventory must decrease by actual product cost
* COGS must increase by actual product cost

Required accounting entry for sale:

1. Revenue entry:
   DR Safe or Customer AR
   CR Sales Revenue

2. Cost entry:
   DR Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
   CR Inventory

IMPORTANT:
Do not calculate profit directly from sale rows only.
Profit must come from Revenue - COGS.

======================

When products are purchased:

Required accounting entry for stock purchase:

DR Inventory
CR Safe or Supplier AP

IMPORTANT:

4) WEIGHTED AVERAGE COST
========================

If the system uses weighted average cost, make it strict and correct.

When purchase happens:

* recalculate average cost correctly

Formula:
New Average Cost =
((Old Qty × Old Avg Cost) + (New Qty × New Purchase Cost))
/
(Old Qty + New Qty)

Store enough data so every sale line knows what cost was used when it was posted.

IMPORTANT:
Do not use current cost later to recalculate old sales.
Each sale must preserve its own historical cost at time of sale.

5) SALES RETURNS
================

When customer returns product:

A) Financial reversal:

* Reverse revenue using RETURN price from original sale
* Refund from Safe OR reduce customer balance

B) Inventory reversal:

* Returned stock must go back to inventory using COST AT TIME OF ORIGINAL SALE
* NOT current sale price
* NOT current average cost unless original cost is unavailable and you explicitly handle that case

Required accounting entry for sales return:

1. Revenue reversal:
   DR Sales Returns / Contra Revenue
   CR Safe or Customer AR

2. Cost reversal:
   DR Inventory
   CR COGS

IMPORTANT:
Returned item must restore inventory by original sale cost, not by selling price.

Example:

* Bought product for 70
* Sold for 100
* Returned by customer

Correct result:

* Revenue reversed by 100
* Inventory restored by 70
* COGS reversed by 70
* Net profit becomes zero for that transaction

This is mandatory.
